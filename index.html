Popular

Morpho peleides

Blue Morpho

Costa Rica's iconic butterfly featuring iridescent blue wings created by light-refracting scales. The brown underside displays cryptic eyespots for predator defense.

Wingspan 12–20 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Monteverde
Large Specimen

Caligo memnon

Giant Owl

Costa Rica's largest butterfly species, distinguished by enormous owl-like eyespots on the ventral wings. Crepuscular habits make wild specimens elusive.

Wingspan 13–20 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Caribbean Lowlands

Greta oto

Glasswing

Remarkable transparent wings provide exceptional camouflage in the cloud forest understory. Wing tissue lacks coloured scales, revealing delicate venation.

Wingspan 5.5–6 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Central Valley

Siproeta stelenes

Malachite

Named for the green mineral, this striking butterfly displays lime-green and black patterning. Active territorial behavior makes them a favorite for live exhibits.

Wingspan 8.5–10 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Guanacaste
Research Grade

Heliconius melpomene

Postman

A key species in mimicry research, displaying warning coloration shared with toxic species. Exhibits remarkable geographic variation in wing patterns.

Wingspan 6–8 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Osa Peninsula

Heliconius sara

Sara Longwing

Elongated forewing shape with deep blue and black coloration. Known for communal roosting behavior, gathering in groups of up to 30 individuals at dusk.

Wingspan 5.5–8 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Tortuguero

Dryas iulia

Julia Heliconian

Also known as the Flame butterfly for its brilliant orange coloration. Exceptionally long-lived for a butterfly, surviving up to 9 months in optimal conditions.

Wingspan 8–9 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Arenal
Limited

Heliconius charithonia

Zebra Longwing

Distinctive black and pale yellow striping. Notable for being one of the few butterflies to feed on pollen, extending lifespan significantly beyond nectar-feeders.

Wingspan 7–10 cm
Family Nymphalidae
Origin Manuel Antonio
